Northern Rocky Mountain Wolf

It can reach a top speed of 50 kilometers per hour.

  • Trinomial Name: Canis lupus irremotus
  • Habitat: America's Rocky Mountains
  • Colors: Coat of white, gray and brown
  • Maximum weight / height: 68 kg / 81 cm

The Rocky Mountain wolf inhabits parts of the western United States and Canada, Alaska, including Unimak Island of the Aleutians. The wolf pack is one of nature's most sophisticated social orders, it is usually a family group of five to eight animals, consisting of a pair of breeding adults and their young of 1 or 2 years old. It feeds on large prey like bison, elk, deer, beaver, pronghorn, and anything else it can catch.
